# The Best Books to Read before a Big Job Interview ...

If you want to ace your job interview, you need to be prepared. In order to get in the right frame of mind, you should try doing a little reading. According to Bustle[,](https://www.bustle.com/articles/131570-the-best-8-books-to-read-before-a-job-interview) here are the **best books to read before a job interview**:

## 1. Ovenman by Jeff Parker

![Ovenman by Jeff Parker](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/xz/pk/gbfpeiiq570262ec6c9bc790777122_600x870.jpg)

This coming of age novel is all about a boy who holds down a kitchen job while navigating the world around him.
Read it here: [amazon.com](http://www.amazon.com?ref=allwomenstalk.com)

## 2. Presence: Bringing Your Boldest Self to Your Biggest Challenges by Dr. Amy Cuddy

![Presence: Bringing Your Boldest Self to Your Biggest Challenges by Dr. Amy Cuddy](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/p5/wh/u555lzwj570262ed6d572707566142_600x930.jpg)

This book will teach you to harness your own personal power so you can ace any interview with zero regrets.
Read it here: [amazon.com](http://www.amazon.com?ref=allwomenstalk.com)

## 3. Hotels of North America by Rick Moody

![Hotels of North America by Rick Moody](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/r4/fr/ivjx9i5w570262edd42a9335877731_600x907.jpg)

Here's an entertaining, but pretty useful, introduction to the ways that technology has shaped and changed the workforce.
Read it here: [amazon.com](http://www.amazon.com?ref=allwomenstalk.com)

## 4. Those Who Leave and Those Who Stay by Elena Ferrante

![Those Who Leave and Those Who Stay by Elena Ferrante](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/je/iv/z60wffic570262ed61733570516917_600x939.jpg)

A heartwarming novel, this book also has a good dose of inspiration for those looking to get a new job and advance their career.
Read it here: [amazon.com](http://www.amazon.com?ref=allwomenstalk.com)

## 5. Bad Feminist by Roxane Gay

![Bad Feminist by Roxane Gay](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/pg/xm/sp031mfd570262ee501cc297790854_600x738.jpg)

This collection of essays touches on a variety of issues that face women today, those that pertain to the workforce and those that influence it.
Read it here: [amazon.com](http://www.amazon.com?ref=allwomenstalk.com)

## 6. Stories in the Worst Way by Gary Lutz

![Stories in the Worst Way by Gary Lutz](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/ai/y9/pcgosi9n570262ecb3237327818833_600x867.jpg)

The short stories in this book might not have a lot to do with getting a job, but it has a cult following so it could come in handy in terms of references and things to talk about.
Read it here: [amazon.com](http://www.amazon.com?ref=allwomenstalk.com)

## 7. Brave Enough by Cheryl Strayed

![Brave Enough by Cheryl Strayed](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/tw/y7/m7ov25bx570262efd2a5d165484675_600x780.jpg)

Going to a big job interview takes a certain amount of bravery and this book will inspire you in the best way.
Read it here: [amazon.com](http://www.amazon.com?ref=allwomenstalk.com)

What other books have you read that would be great to prep for an interview?
