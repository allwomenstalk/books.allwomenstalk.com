29 Books That Will Fuel Your Wanderlust ...

The Alchemist by Paulo Coelho • Eat, Pray, Love by Elizabeth Gilbert • Stamboul Train by Graham Greene • As I Walked out One Summer Morning by Laurie Lee • Istanbul: Memoires and the City by Orhan Pamuk • More ...

By Neecey

A book has the power to transport you from your everyday life into other worlds. Books can take you into fantasy lands where your imagination can run riot or they can whisk you off to places all around the world, to cultures and people very different to your neighborhood. These are some books that will inflame your wanderlust so much that you better have a bag packed ready.

1. The Alchemist by Paulo Coelho

A spellbinding journey from Spain to Egypt that will give you itchy feet as you turn the pages. An absolute must read.

Buy at: amazon.com

2. Eat, Pray, Love by Elizabeth Gilbert

An inspirational and uplifting journey of self discovery that takes the reader through India, Italy and Bali. Full of introspective intrigue.

Buy at: amazon.com

3. Stamboul Train by Graham Greene

A 1930’s thriller that takes place aboard the iconic Orient Express as it rolls through Europe. Mystery and wanderlust all in one, perfect for curious travel lovers.

Buy at: amazon.com

4. As I Walked out One Summer Morning by Laurie Lee

A wonderful book that showcases the vibrancy, pride and humility in traditional Spanish life. Full of humor and life, it will make your ache for the Mediterranean!

Buy at: amazon.com

5. Istanbul: Memoires and the City by Orhan Pamuk

A wonderful memoir about the author’s life in Istanbul where he still lives in the very apartment building in which he was raised. A beautiful and fascinating insight.

Buy at: amazon.com

6. The Geography of Bliss: One Grump’s Search for the Happiest Places in the World by Eric Weiner

This unique book is a comedy, self help guide and foreign affairs commentary all in one as the writer travels from America to India to Iceland in search of true happiness.

Buy at: amazon.com

7. Life of Pi by Yann Martel

Who would have thought the story of a zookeeper’s son stranded at sea with a tiger would make you want to explore the world so much? Well, it does, it really does!

Buy at: amazon.com

8. Love with a Chance of Drowning by Torre DeRoche

A captivating tale of new love in an exotic and exciting destination, combined with a truly once in a lifetime kind of adventure, this novel will have you packing your bags the second you’ve finished!

Buy at: amazon.com

9. My Documents by Alejandro Zambra

This book is a collection of short stories about life in Chile both during and in the aftermath of the Pinochet regime. A stunning read, you won’t be able to put it down.

Buy at: amazon.com

10. The Lost Girls: Three Friends. Four Continents. One Unconventional Detour around the World. by Jennifer Baggett

An inspiring read about three New York City girls who decide to leave their hectic lives behind and go on a yearlong around the world adventure. It will definitely give you ideas!

Buy at: amazon.com

11. A Field Guide to Getting Lost by Rebecca Solnit

A combination of travel and history, this book will give you a sparkle of wanderlust but also a healthy dose of philosophy too.

Buy at: amazon.com

12. The Scapegoat by Daphne Du Maurier

A sweeping tale about a French aristocrat and English man who switch places after a chance encounter a railway station. It will spark the need for some travel excitement in your own life.

Buy at: amazon.com

13. The Paris Wife by Paula McLain

Exploring the interesting relationship between writer Ernest Hemingway and his wife Hadley, The Paris Wife is full of glorious detail about the stunning city in which they lived.

Buy at: amazon.com

14. Black Lamb and Grey Falcon by Rebecca West

This 1942 book is regarded as one of the very best for providing a captivating account of life in the former state of Yugoslavia. Really interesting.

Buy at: amazon.com

15. Travels with Charley: in Search of America by John Steinbeck

The iconic writer travels across America with his loyal dog Charley in an attempt the rediscover the magic of his homeland.

Buy at: amazon.com

16. The Great Railway Bazaar by Paul Theroux

A deeply personal account of the author’s journey through Asia that encapsulates the sights, sounds, tastes of cultures of the continent. A real page-turner.

Buy at: amazon.com

17. The Blue Sky by Galsan Tschinag

A fascinating read about the Altai Mountains in Mongolia, and how the local residents were forced to enter the modern world.

Buy at: amazon.com

18. In Patagonia by Bruce Chatwin

Full of incredible imagery and sure to fuel your wanderlust, this story of a man travelling to a remote country in search of a strange and mythical beast will delight and inspire in all the best ways.

Buy at: amazon.com

19. Up in the Air by Walter Kirn

This witty and engaging novel about a man who spends most of his life on planes will give you a whole new perspective on flying. It’s much more than just getting from A to B!

Buy at: amazon.com

20. Notes from a Small Island by Bill Bryson

The godfather of travel writing provides a sharp and hilarious tale of an American moving back home after living in Britain. A funny exploration of different cultures.

Buy at: amazon.com

21. The Twenty-One Balloons by William Pene De Bois

This beautiful novel tells the whimsical story of a professor who flies in his own hot air balloon to an imaginary island. Let the wanderlust consume you as you turn each page.

Buy at: amazon.com

22. Homage to Catalonia by George Orwell

This book is about the legendary author’s experiences of life in Spain during the Spanish Civil War. Full of history and endlessly interesting.

Buy at: amazon.com

23. Shantaram: a Novel by Gregory David Roberts

This is a riveting tale that follows three different people through the city of Bombay, giving insights in to different aspects such as gangsters, holy men and prostitution.

Buy at: amazon.com

24. On the Road by Jack Kerouac

An American classic about five friends who take a road trip together across the States in the 1950s. Full of young angst and self discovery, it’s a real must read.

Buy at: amazon.com

25. The Sun Also Rises by Ernest Hemingway

Hemingway’s first really big novel, it tells the story of a group of expats who travel together from Paris to Spain to watch a bullfight. A really interesting mix of cultures is described.

Buy at: amazon.com

26. My Brilliant Friend by Elena Ferrante

A beautifully told story about the lives of two friends who grow up together in a small neighborhood on the cusp of the Italian city of Naples. Stunning imagery throughout.

Buy at: amazon.com

27. Crowfall by Shanta Gokhale

A captivating novel about a diverse group of characters during a single year in the Indian city of Mumbai.

Buy at: amazon.com

28. Summer Sisters by Judy Blume

A touching novel about two friends who travel between Martha’s Vineyard and New York City whilst addressing some long overdue problems in their relationship.

Buy at: amazon.com

29. A Walk in the Woods by Bill Bryson

Recently made in to a film, this book will leave you wanting to hike the Appalachian Trail more than ever before!

Buy at: amazon.com
