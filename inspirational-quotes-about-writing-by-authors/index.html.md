# 7 Inspirational Quotes about Writing by Authors ...

When you're running low on inspiration, reading quotes about writing by authors can spark your creativity. From Stephen King to John Green, these authors have great advice for budding writers. Since they're so successful, you might as well listen to what they have to say. The following quotes about writing by authors should get your creative juices flowing.

## 1. Social Anxiety

Neil Gaiman discusses why writers don't have to feel comfortable in social situations. He has said, "It's not a bad thing for a writer not to feel at home. Writers - we're much more comfortable at parties standing in the corner watching everybody else having a good time than we are mingling." In order to write well, you have to be an observer of the world. In order to get inspiration for a story, you have to watch the world unfold around you, or you could read some helpful quotes about writing by authors.

## 2. Read, Read, Read

J.K. Rowling said, "The most important thing is to read as much as you can, like I did. It will give you an understanding of what makes good writing and it will enlarge your vocabulary." In order to become a better writer, you have to read as much as possible. You can't perfect your craft if you're not constantly aiming to improve. By reading fantastic authors, you'll learn new ways to enhance your stories. By reading horribly written novels, you'll have an idea of what not to do.

## 3. Bare Your Soul

Virginia Wolf said, "Every secret of a writer's soul, every experience of his life, every quality of his mind is written large in his works." Since you can't write what you don't know, it's quite clear that your work is going to be a mirror of your life. You can't be afraid to let your soul enter your work, because that's the entire point of writing. You want your stories to contain truth, and to do that, you have to express your emotions. If you don't want to insert a part of yourself into your writing, then why bother to pick up a pen?

## 4. Believe in Your Work

John Steinbeck said, "The writer must believe that what he is doing is the most important thing in the world. And he must hold to this illusion even when he knows it is not true." If you don't cherish your work, no one else will. It's your job to love what you're writing. If you don't think that what you're trying to convey is important, then it will show in your work.

## 5. Spill Your Emotions

Ernest Hemingway said, "There is nothing to writing. All you do is sit down at a typewriter and bleed." Even though typewriters are outdated, the concept still stands. In order to produce the best work possible, you have to let all of your emotions bleed onto the paper. Don't hold anything back.

## 6. Work Hard

Stephen King said, "Talent is cheaper than table salt. What separates the talented individual from the successful one is a lot of hard work." You can't expect success to be handed to you. You have to work your hardest in order to accomplish what you want to accomplish. Don't procrastinate and don't be a slacker.

## 7. Write and Write Some More

John Green said, "Read a lot. Read broadly. Tell stories to your friends, and pay attention to when they get bored. Write a lot." If you want to get better at something, you need to practice. Show your work to others and see how they react to it.

Whenever you sit down to write, remember the words of these famous authors. Do you like to write? What's your favorite genre?
