# 9 Crucial Books to Read before You Start Your Own Business ...

If your biggest dream is to have your own business and you don’t plan on enrolling in business school, then I must tell you that there are a couple of books to read before you start your own business. There are a lot of famous CEOs that have never been to college, yet they are among the most powerful and influential people in the entire world. I’m not saying you shouldn’t go to college and get a degree; I just want to motivate you to get better at what you do in order to make your business successful. Here are a few important and useful books to read before you start your own business:

## 1. Million Dollar Consulting – Alan Weiss

_ **On Amazon at:** amazon.com_

This is definitely one of the most important books to read before you start your own business. It provides a remarkable overview of the business of solo consulting and it has earned Alan Weiss the title of “Rock Star of Consulting”. His advice is very practical and it covers the fundamentals from setting up your office to writing proposals. It provides step-by-step usable advice and it’s a must-read if you’re trying to improve your business skills.

## 2. The Business Start-up Kit – Steven D. Strauss

![The Business Start-up Kit – Steven D. Strauss](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/a9/yv/zx/aomy3m94_260x327.jpg)

**On Amazon at:** [amazon.com](https://web.archive.org/web/https://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0793160278/lifehack-20)

This is an indispensable how-to guide because it really offers you everything you need to know on how to start your own business. Strauss writes in a very friendly and entertaining way and it gives you a lot of tips on how to grow your business without a lot of seed money. He also offers you a lot of useful ideas on how to succeed in business and explains what works and what doesn’t work in start-ups.

## 3. Start Your Own Business – Rieva Lesonsky

_ **On Amazon at:** amazon.com_

Every young entrepreneur should read this book, especially since it bears the following tag-line: “The only start-up book you’ll ever need”. You can find now the fourth edition of this book which has been a huge success; it sold more than 200,000 copies. The author says that this book is designed “to help you navigate your own path to business ownership”, so why not listen to her advice and read it before you plan on starting your own business?

## 4. Will It Fly? – Thomas K. McKnight

_ **On Amazon at:** amazon.com_

This wonderful and extremely helpful book is number 1 ranked of top 5 Must Read Business Books and it basically teaches you how to know if your business idea has wings, if it will take off or fall flat. It has a lot of very practical advice which is easy to apply, it provides detail explanation and it helps you evaluate a business concept very quickly. It’s written by a very inspiring mentor who will help you make your idea come to life and fulfill your dream of being the owner of a very successful business.

## 5. The Art of the Start – Guy Kawasaki

_ **On Amazon at:** amazon.com_

This is definitely one of the most important books you should read if you want to start your own business. It’s well-written, it’s pretty easy to read and it contains a lot of real-life examples. It’s a very good book, it’s very helpful and I can even say that it’s quite indispensable for every young entrepreneur. The fact that the author uses humor to explain different things makes it even more fun to read, while being extremely educational at the same time.

## 6. The Business Planning Guide – David H. Bangs Jr

_ **On Amazon at:** amazon.com_

A lot of people have said that this book gives a great background on how to present your ideas while writing up your business plan, so why not give it a try. It provides sound advice on how to convince others that your business is worth supporting. I could even say that it’s a must for beginners, since it is full of tips and tricks from the author’s experience and he is a former banker and entrepreneur.

## 7. Startup from the Ground up – Cynthia Kocialski

_ **On Amazon at:** amazon.com_

This is definitely one of the books every young entrepreneur should read, since it provides a lot of very useful advice on how to transform your idea into a business. You will also learn how to recruit and hire a very effective team, how to seek funding and basically how to build a successful business. I highly recommend you to read this book because it offers you some extremely helpful information on how to build your business from nothing and because it tells you what to expect, and what to do when you’re trying to make your business successful.

## 8. The Fire Starter Sessions – Danielle LaPorte

_ **On Amazon at:** amazon.com_

Just like it says in the title, it’s a soulful and practical guide to creating success on your own terms. It actually makes something pretty difficult look easy; it’s very well-written and very inspiring. It’s full of personal anecdotes, it’s a pleasurable self-help read that you should definitely consider if you dream of starting your own business and making it a real success.

## 9. Lucky or Smart? – Bo Peabody

![Lucky or Smart? – Bo Peabody](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/qq/yx/lx/l5tkfstj_212x346.jpg)

**On Amazon at:** [amazon.com](https://web.archive.org/web/https://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/1439210101/lifehack-20)

This is a very fun book to read. It’s very entertaining, it’s filled with anecdotes and it also contains a lot of practical advice. This is definitely a must-read for anyone who dreams of becoming an entrepreneur; it’s very helpful, very motivational and very positive. So, just give it a try; you might find yourself enjoying it!

There are a lot of things you should keep in mind if you decide to become an entrepreneur and that’s why, you should educate yourself first. Just read a lot of books on this matter and you could even take some courses if you think it will help you turn your business into a success. Have you read any of these books? Did you find them helpful? Do you know any other books someone should read before they decide to start their own business? Do tell!
